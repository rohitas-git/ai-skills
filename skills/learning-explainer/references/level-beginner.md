# Level: Beginner (Absolute Beginners / New Learners)

## Purpose
Build foundational understanding for people who are completely new to the topic. Focus on clear foundations, basic vocabulary, and confidence building.

## Audience Characteristics
- Little to no prior knowledge
- May feel intimidated by the topic
- Needs explicit definitions of every new term
- Benefits from repetition and multiple examples
- Often non-native speakers or students starting a subject

## Goals
- Remove fear and build confidence
- Introduce core vocabulary with clear definitions
- Create the first solid mental model
- Use simple but slightly more mature language than Child level

## Vocabulary & Style Guidelines
- Define every important word the first time it appears
- Short to medium sentences
- Concrete examples from daily life
- Gentle repetition of key ideas
- Encouraging tone ("This is easier than it sounds...")

## Recommended Structure
1. **One-sentence overview**
2. **Key terms defined simply**
3. **Core idea with one good analogy**
4. **Concrete everyday example**
5. **Common misconception to avoid**
6. **Simple check-in question**

## Example (Photosynthesis)
"Photosynthesis is how plants make their own food using sunlight.

Key idea: Plants take sunlight, water from the ground, and a gas called carbon dioxide from the air, then turn them into sugar (their food) and oxygen (which we breathe).

Simple analogy: Think of a plant like a solar-powered kitchen. The leaves are the chefs, sunlight is the energy, and they cook using water and air.

Example: A sunflower uses this process to grow tall and produce seeds. Without photosynthesis, there would be no food for animals or humans."

## Mental Model Suggestion
Clear, relatable analogy (e.g., "solar kitchen", "tiny factory inside the leaf").

## Diagram Guidance
Simple labeled diagrams. Use arrows showing inputs → plant → outputs (sugar + oxygen).

## CLT Notes
Focus on reducing extraneous load through clear definitions and one strong analogy. Build germane load with one worked example.

## Trigger Phrases
"beginner level", "absolute beginner", "new to this", "explain from scratch", "I know nothing about..."

## Best Practices
- Always define terms before using them.
- Use one strong analogy per explanation.
- End with encouragement and an offer to go deeper.
