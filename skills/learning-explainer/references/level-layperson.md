# Level: Layperson (Educated General Audience)

## Purpose
Explain concepts clearly and engagingly to people with general knowledge but no special expertise in the topic. Balance accuracy with accessibility.

## Audience Characteristics
- Has general education and life experience
- Comfortable with some abstract ideas
- Appreciates relatable real-world examples
- Wants to understand "why it matters"
- May have heard some terms but not deeply

## Goals
- Give an accurate but intuitive understanding
- Connect the concept to everyday life or bigger picture
- Use natural, conversational language
- Show practical relevance

## Vocabulary & Style Guidelines
- Mostly everyday language with light use of necessary terms (always explained)
- Mix of concrete examples and simple abstractions
- Engaging, slightly conversational tone
- Use "you" and real-world scenarios

## Recommended Structure
1. **Hook** (why this matters in daily life or news)
2. **Clear core explanation**
3. **Relatable analogy or comparison**
4. **Real-world example or application**
5. **Common question or misconception addressed**
6. **Bigger picture** (why it’s interesting or important)

## Example (Photosynthesis)
"Photosynthesis is the process that allows plants to create their own food from sunlight, water, and air — and it’s the foundation of almost all life on Earth.

In simple terms: Plants capture sunlight in their leaves and use it to turn carbon dioxide (from the air) and water (from the soil) into sugar and oxygen. The sugar feeds the plant, and the oxygen is released into the air for us to breathe.

Think of it like a natural solar panel combined with a tiny chemical factory inside every green leaf.

Real-world impact: Without photosynthesis, there would be no plants, no food chain, and very little oxygen. It’s also why forests and oceans are so important for fighting climate change — they act as giant carbon sinks."

## Mental Model Suggestion
"Natural solar-powered factory" or "Earth’s lungs and food factory".

## Diagram Guidance
Clean diagram showing inputs (sunlight, CO₂, H₂O) → leaf → outputs (sugar/glucose + O₂). Simple flowchart style.

## CLT Notes
Good balance of intrinsic load. Use one strong mental model and real-world relevance to increase germane processing.

## Trigger Phrases
"layperson", "normal person", "everyday explanation", "for a general audience", "explain simply but accurately"

## Best Practices
- Connect to things people already care about (food, air, climate, health).
- Avoid both oversimplification and unnecessary jargon.
- End with why the topic is worth knowing.
