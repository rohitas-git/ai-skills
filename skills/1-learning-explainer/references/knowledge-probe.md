# User Knowledge Probe & Clarification Questions

## Purpose
Start every explanation by quickly assessing the user's current knowledge level, goals, and preferences. This allows the skill to adapt the depth, style, and focus of the response for maximum relevance and minimal cognitive mismatch.

## Why It Matters
- Prevents explaining at the wrong level (too simple or too advanced)
- Uncovers specific interests or pain points
- Builds rapport and shows the user they are being understood
- Improves efficiency by focusing on what the user actually needs

## Standard Opening Question
"On a scale of 1-10, how familiar are you with [topic]? Any specific background, focus areas, or questions you have?"

## Recommended Follow-up Questions
Use these as needed, depending on the user's first answer:

**Level & Depth**
- "Would you like a simple overview first, or are you comfortable going deeper?"
- "Do you have a preferred level? (e.g., beginner, layperson, specialist, academic)"
- "Would you like me to start broad and then go deeper on specific parts?"

**Focus & Goals**
- "What are you hoping to get out of this explanation? (e.g., basic understanding, practical application, exam prep, 1-research context)"
- "Are there particular aspects you're most interested in?"
- "Is there anything specific you already know or want to avoid?"

**Format Preferences**
- "Would you prefer a compact summary or a more detailed breakdown?"
- "Do you want mental models and diagrams?"
- "Would you like examples from daily life, work, or technical contexts?"

**Special Modes**
- "Would you like me to use Cognitive Load Theory principles to structure the explanation?" (only if relevant)
- "Should I include a progressive deep-dive option?"
- "Do you want me to generate any visuals or diagrams?"

## Best Practices
- Ask the knowledge probe **at the very beginning**, before giving any explanation.
- Keep the initial probe short and friendly.
- Adapt your language in the probe itself to the apparent level of the user.
- If the user gives a vague answer, ask one clarifying follow-up.
- Remember the user's stated level and preferences throughout the conversation.
- Re-probe if the conversation shifts to a very different sub-topic.

## Example Interaction
**AI**: On a scale of 1-10, how familiar are you with photosynthesis? Any specific background or focus?

**User**: Around 4. I'm a gardener and want to understand why my plants aren't growing well in shade.

**AI**: Got it. I'll explain it at a practical layperson level with examples relevant to gardening and light conditions. Would you like me to include simple diagrams?
