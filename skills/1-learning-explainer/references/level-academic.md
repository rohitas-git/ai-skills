# Level: Academic / Graduate

## Purpose
Provide a rigorous, scholarly explanation suitable for students, researchers, or academics. Emphasize theory, evidence, methodology, and critical thinking.

## Audience Characteristics
- Strong foundational knowledge in the broader field
- Comfortable with abstract reasoning and formal concepts
- Expects citations, evidence, and methodological awareness
- Interested in debates, limitations, and open questions
- Values precision and intellectual honesty

## Goals
- Deliver accurate, well-supported explanations
- Situate the concept within broader theoretical frameworks
- Discuss key literature and methodological approaches
- Highlight limitations, controversies, and open problems

## Vocabulary & Style Guidelines
- Precise, formal but readable academic tone
- Use correct technical terminology
- Reference concepts, theories, and researchers where relevant
- Balanced and objective

## Recommended Structure
1. **Precise definition** + historical/theoretical context
2. **Core principles / mechanisms** with supporting logic
3. **Key evidence and methods** used to study it
4. **Major theoretical frameworks** or models
5. **Limitations, debates, and critiques**
6. **Current open questions** and 1-research directions

## Example (Photosynthesis – Academic level)
"Photosynthesis is the light-driven reduction of CO₂ to carbohydrates coupled with the oxidation of water, occurring in oxygenic photoautotrophs.

The process consists of two main stages: (1) light-dependent reactions in thylakoid membranes that generate ATP and NADPH via photosynthetic electron transport, and (2) light-independent (Calvin-Benson-Bassham) cycle in the stroma that fixes CO₂ into triose phosphates.

Key theoretical frameworks include the Z-scheme of electron transport and the Farquhar–von Caemmerer–Berry (FvCB) model of C3 photosynthesis. Methodological approaches range from chlorophyll fluorescence analysis and gas exchange measurements to structural biology of photosystems and genetic manipulation of Rubisco.

Major debates include the efficiency limits of C3 vs C4/CAM pathways, the role of photorespiration under future climate scenarios, and the quantum yield of photosystem II under stress. Open questions involve improving photosynthetic efficiency for crop yield improvement and understanding the evolution of oxygenic photosynthesis."

## Mental Model Suggestion
"Two-stage biochemical engine with light harvesting, electron transport chain, and carbon fixation modules" or "Z-scheme energy staircase".

## Diagram Guidance
Detailed Z-scheme diagram, Calvin cycle pathway, or comparison of C3/C4 anatomy and biochemistry.

## CLT Notes
Higher intrinsic load is expected. Use clear structure, explicit frameworks, and signaling (headings, bold key terms) to manage load.

## Trigger Phrases
"academic level", "graduate", "scholarly", "university level", "rigorous explanation", "with theory and evidence"

## Best Practices
- Always ground claims in evidence or established theory.
- Acknowledge limitations and competing views.
- Suggest key papers or reviews when relevant.
- End with current frontiers or open problems.
